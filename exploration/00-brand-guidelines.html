Design Philosophy

Pulse is built for operators, consultants, and creative business founders who win work through conversations. The design should feel like a system that celebrates momentum while keeping users grounded in practical execution.

Core Principles

Rhythm & Momentum: Visual language mirrors the heartbeat of consistent outreach. Every interaction feels intentional, not decorative.

70% Creative, 30% Minimalist: The audience is designers, agency founders, and business developers who value aesthetics but prize clarity. We lean creative but don't sacrifice usability.

Typography as Hero: Bold, editorial type carries emotional weight. Space Grotesk (70s energy) or Outfit (modern editorial) — decision pending.

Celebration Moments Matter: When users hit milestones, the design should feel like they won something. Gradient text, glowing badges, metric cards that pop.

Typography

DECISION PENDING

Two directions being tested. Both bold, editorial, contemporary. One leans 70s (Space Grotesk), one leans modern (Outfit). Both paired with DM Sans for supporting type.

Decision Matrix: Space Grotesk vs. Outfit

Attribute Space Grotesk (A) Outfit (B)
Weight Chunky, condensed, high contrast Clean, geometric, tight letterspacing
Energy 70s Swiss editorial (Sight and Sound vibes) Contemporary editorial (future-facing, no nostalgia)
Legibility Slightly lighter weight, sharper, more unique Tighter spacing, bolder appearance, slightly more generic
Feedback Preferred by Gareth Evans (more unique, system-like) Preferred by second reviewer (bolder, cleaner look)
Use Case Landing page, marketing, celebration moments Dashboard, UI, consistency-focused contexts

Micro-Interactions

IN DEVELOPMENT

Motion carries emotional weight. Every interaction is intentional, not decorative. Currently in research phase.

Planned Micro-Interactions

Metric Card Pulse: Celebration badge pulses in rhythm with a heartbeat metaphor. Glow extends slightly on pulse.

Scanning Line Effect (Reference): WebGPU scanning effect with depth maps. Sweeping line across metric card outlines, revealing neon glow as it passes. See reference →

Gradient Text Animation: Hero headline with animated gradient. Neon colors shift subtly, creating a sense of movement and energy.

Campaign Card Hover: Border shifts from hot pink to cyan on hover. Background subtly brightens.

Animation Philosophy

All motion should feel intentional and rewarding. No arbitrary animations. If it moves, it should communicate something or celebrate a moment.

Voice & Tone

DEVELOPING

Opinionated, playful, character-driven. Not false-American SaaS. Inspired by Oatly, Monzo, Mailchimp, Innocent.

Personality Packs (Future)

Default pack: Friendly. Future packs will include Direct, Dry, Playful. When personality packs are developed, design aesthetic may shift per pack (typography, color emphasis, micro-interactions).

Note: Currently locked on one default aesthetic. Personality pack visual variations are a future exploration.

Exploration Archive

What we've tested, what we've rejected, and why. Documenting the design journey.

Palettes Explored

Electric Dreams
LOCKED. Deep purple + neon cyan, hot pink, orange. Unapologetic and vibrant.
Quiet Luxury
Explored but rejected. Too understated for the brand energy we're going for. Sophisticated but felt muted.
Neo-Naturalism
Explored but rejected. Warm terracottas felt too organic for a SaaS dashboard. Right palette, wrong product.

Typography Tested

Space Grotesk + DM Sans
70s editorial energy. Chunky, condensed, playful. Feedback: More unique, system-like, sharper.
Outfit + DM Sans
Modern editorial. Clean, geometric, tight. Feedback: Bolder, tighter, slightly more generic but very polished.

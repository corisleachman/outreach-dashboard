# Pulse. — LinkedIn Deep Dive

**Strategic Vision:** Dual-purpose system = sales channel + network broadening vehicle  
**Goal:** 8–12 LinkedIn signups/week by June 2026  
**Approach:** High-velocity outreach (100–150 connections/week) + warm follow-up sequences (DM + email) + manual takeover on response  
**Timeline:** Weeks 1–4 of June (setup + testing), Weeks 1–8 (June–July execution and optimization)

---

## ICP & Targeting

### Primary Audiences (All Regions: UK + US)

**Audience 1: Advertising Agencies**
- Job titles: "New Business Director," "Head of New Business," "BD Manager," "Growth Director," "Account Director"
- Company size: 10–500 people
- Geography: London, New York (primary); expand later
- Pain point: Struggling to scale outreach consistently; manual follow-ups break momentum
- Motivation: They do cold outreach themselves OR manage team doing it

**Audience 2: Recruitment/Staffing Firms**
- Job titles: "Recruitment Consultant," "BD Lead," "Account Manager," "Owner" (1–10 person firms)
- Company size: 1–10 people (small, scrappy, lean)
- Geography: UK + US
- Pain point: Time-intensive prospecting; hard to track follow-ups across multiple targets
- Motivation: Direct decision-maker; can buy independently

**Audience 3: Fractional Operators & Interim Leaders**
- Job titles: "Fractional COO," "Interim CEO," "Executive Coach," "Consultant"
- Company size: Solo or small partnerships
- Geography: UK + US
- Pain point: Managing multiple clients, need to stay top-of-mind without 5 hours/week overhead
- Motivation: Understand operational efficiency; early adopters of tools

### Secondary Benefit
- **Network Expansion:** Each outreach = opportunity to connect with industry peer, regardless of immediate conversion
- **Positioning:** You're the founder solving a real problem in your space (credibility boost)

## Multi-Channel Sequence (Visual Flow)

```
Day 1: Send 100–150 connection requests (spread across week)
        ├─ Each request may include optional CONNECTION MESSAGE (variant A, B, C, or D)
        └─ Target: 5–8% acceptance rate = 5–12 acceptances/day

Day 4 (after acceptance): Dripify sends DM #1 (warm, question-based, build trust)
        ├─ Variant 1: Recruitment/staffing focus
        ├─ Variant 2: Agency/growth focus
        └─ Target: Track response rate (we'll measure + iterate)

Day 3–4 (simultaneous or overlapping): Send EMAIL (personally warm, short, no jargon)
        ├─ Variant A: Recruitment/staffing audience
        ├─ Variant B: Agency/growth audience
        └─ Target: Track open rate, click rate

Day 8–9 (if no DM response): Dripify sends DM #2 (references email, soft reminder)
        └─ Target: Catch people who saw email but didn't click

Day 13–14 (if still no response): Dripify sends DM #3 (final touch, soft CTA)
        └─ If they engage at ANY point, you take over manually
```

## DM #1 (Day 4 After Connection Acceptance)

**Strategy:** Question-based, build trust first, no hard sell. Acknowledge them, ask about their situation.

**Tone:** Professional but warm. Direct and confident. No fluff. Show genuine curiosity about their pain.

**Variant 1 (Recruitment/Staffing):**
```
Thanks for connecting! Since you're managing prospecting at [company], 
I'm curious—what's your biggest pain point right now? Is it time? 
Consistency in follow-ups? Or something else?

I ask because I built Pulse. to solve exactly that. But first, 
what would actually matter to you?
```

**Variant 2 (Agency/Growth):**
```
Thanks for connecting! I'm guessing you're doing cold outreach 
(or managing a team that is). What's the biggest friction point 
for you—is it the volume, the follow-ups, or tracking who's actually engaged?

I built Pulse. to solve those, but curious what YOUR biggest blocker is first.
```

**Variant 3 (Fractional/Interim):**
```
Thanks for connecting! Since you're juggling multiple clients/projects, 
I'm curious—how do you stay on top of prospect follow-ups without it 
eating your whole week?

I built Pulse. to save that time. Wondering if it'd be useful for your world.
```

**Usage:** 
- Select variant based on audience type (pulled from their profile/headline)
- Send via Dripify on Day 4
- Wait for response; if they engage, you take over

## Decision Rules & Optimization

### If Acceptance Rate <3%

**Diagnosis:** Connection request message (or angle) isn't resonating.

**Action:**
1. Pause for 2–3 days
2. Change CONNECTION MESSAGE to different variant or angle
3. Resume at 15 requests/day (slower) to test new message
4. Measure acceptance rate for 50 new requests
5. If still <3%, try "bare request" (no message) on next batch

### If DM #1 Response Rate <5%

**Diagnosis:** DM copy isn't compelling enough, or timing is off.

**Action:**
1. Review DM responses: What are people saying (if anything)?
2. Try different DM #1 variant next batch
3. Or shift timing: Send on Day 3 instead of Day 4 (test different windows)
4. If response rate stays low, reassess: Are we connecting with the right people?

### If Email Click Rate <2%

**Diagnosis:** Email subject line, copy, or CTA isn't clear.

**Action:**
1. A/B test email subject lines (use outreach. tool to send variants)
2. Try shorter email (2 sentences instead of 3 paragraphs)
3. Or try different CTA (direct beta link vs. "reply to chat")
4. Measure next batch of 50 emails

### If Zero Signups After 2 Weeks

**Diagnosis:** Something structural is broken (wrong audience, bad message, no follow-up clarity).

**Action:**
1. Pull 3–5 recent conversations (look at DMs, replies)
2. Do 1:1 call with someone who engaged but didn't sign up (find blocker)
3. Fix messaging based on feedback
4. Resume outreach

### If Acceptance Rate >8%

**Diagnosis:** Great! Your messaging is working.

**Action:**
1. Keep the variant that's winning
2. Scale up: 150 → 200 requests/week
3. Don't tinker—compound what's working

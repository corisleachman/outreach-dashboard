Pricing strategy
Under consideration — not yet decided
✅ Decisions locked — wireframes updated
Design principle: Every pricing and conversion decision is grounded in market evidence and historical SaaS research — not opinion. The question is always: what has the market demonstrated works?
Key evidence bases: peak-end rule (Kahneman), loss aversion 2× coefficient (Kahneman & Tversky), compromise effect (Huber et al. 1982), Dropbox referral data, Stripe checkout friction research.
Proposed three-tier model
Tier Indicative price Role Psychology
Free £0 Entry point, constrained Gets people in the door
Pro Light Most popular ~£7/mo annual
~£12/mo monthly		 The target — compromise effect pulls users here Middle option feels like the rational choice
Pro Plus ~£19/mo annual
~£29/mo monthly		 Expensive anchor — makes Pro Light feel obvious Most users look and think "I don't need all that"
Areas that would need to change
1 · Tier limits
New limits needed for campaigns, prospects, AI drafts, and sequences at each of three tiers. Currently only two tiers are defined. This is the most important decision — everything else flows from it.
2 · Pricing page
3-column plans page with "Most popular" badge on Pro Light. Pro Plus priced as an anchor. Animated toggle already built — needs a third column added. New checkout states for each paid tier.
3 · Limit walls — all 9 surfaces
Every wall currently offers Free → Pro. With three tiers, each wall must know the user's current tier and offer the correct next step up. Free users see "upgrade to Pro Light." Pro Light users hitting their higher limits see "upgrade to Pro Plus."

Affected walls: prospect soft nudge (80), prospect hard wall (100), campaign limit, AI drafts, auto-scheduling, sequence steps 2+, bulk send, personality packs, follow-up tab actions.
4 · Settings — Plan & Billing
Currently 3 billing states (Free / Pro Light trial / Pro). Needs 4 states: Free / Pro Light trial / Pro Light paid / Pro Plus paid. Change Plan modal needs to handle all four upgrade and downgrade directions.
5 · Upgrade moments spec
The entire spec is written for a two-tier world. The tier gating summary table needs rewriting to show what each upgrade moment type shows to each of the three tiers. Trial lifecycle (Type 4) applies to Pro Light trial — does Pro Plus also get a trial?
6 · Supabase schema
The tier enum in the users table needs a new value: pro_plus. Small migration, but RLS policies and TypeScript types reference tier values throughout.
7 · Stripe
Currently 2 price IDs (Pro annual + monthly). Three tiers = 4 price IDs. New products in Stripe dashboard, new env vars, webhook handler updated to map each to the correct tier.
8 · Feature gating logic
Every tier check in the codebase changes from binary (free / not-free) to three-way logic. Features unlocked at Pro Light stay unlocked at Pro Plus. Features exclusive to Pro Plus need new gating.
9 · Copy throughout the product
"Pro feature" → "Pro Light feature" or "Pro Light & Pro Plus feature". Trial banners, upgrade CTAs, settings labels, invoice descriptions. Phase 2.12 microcopy system makes this straightforward — all copy is in JSON config.
10 · Wireframes
6 wireframes need updating: pricing-upgrade.html (3 columns), upgrade-moments.html (tier-aware CTAs), settings-v2.html (Pro Plus billing state), campaign-view-v3.html (locked labels), outreach-wizard-v3.html (trial copy), dashboard-home.html (plan badge).
Questions to answer before speccing
  • What are the actual limits at each tier? (campaigns, prospects, AI drafts/month) — this drives everything
  • Does Pro Plus have a trial, or is it only reachable by upgrading from Pro Light?
  • Can users go Free → Pro Plus directly, skipping Pro Light?
  • What features are Pro Plus exclusive that aren't in Pro Light? (If nothing compelling, the anchor effect fails)
  • What are the actual price points? The spread needs to make Pro Light feel like a bargain vs Pro Plus
  • Downgrade rules: if a Pro Plus user downgrades to Pro Light, what happens to data above Pro Light limits?
Estimated effort once decisions are made: ~25–40 hours engineering. Full detail in .planning/PRICING-THREE-TIER-IMPACT.md in the app repo.

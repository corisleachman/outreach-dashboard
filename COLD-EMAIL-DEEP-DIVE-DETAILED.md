# Pulse. — Cold Email Deep Dive

**Strategic Vision:** Multi-temperature outreach using Sales Navigator lists at 4 different relationship temperatures (personal → ex-colleague → LinkedIn-connected → completely cold), with coordinated timing across LinkedIn DM + cold email channels.

**Goal:** 2–4 cold email signups/week by July 2026  
**Approach:** 50 emails/week from Sales Nav exports via Lix → segmented by relationship level → 3-email sequences (Days 0, 5, 10) + parallel LinkedIn DM outreach (Day 4) on same prospects  
**Timeline:** Weeks 1–4 of June (setup + testing), Weeks 1–8 (June–July execution and optimization)

---

## Overview: 4 Warm-to-Cold Segments

You'll build **4 distinct segments** based on relationship temperature. Each gets the **same 3-email sequence structure** but **different opening copy** tailored to your relationship.

| Segment | Relationship | Email #1 Opening | Temperature | Volume Target |
|---------|--------------|------------------|-------------|----------------|
| **A** | Personal (know well, recent contact) | "Hey [Name], it's been a while. I built something..." | Warmest | 5–10/week |
| **B** | Ex-colleague (worked together, haven't spoken recently) | "Been a while. Made something you might want." | Warm | 10–15/week |
| **C** | LinkedIn-connected (never met, connected via Sales Nav) | "Saw your profile. This seems relevant to what you do." | Cool | 15–20/week |
| **D** | Completely cold (no prior connection) | "Most people doing what you do struggle with [pain]. I built Pulse. to fix that." | Coldest | 5–10/week |

**Total: 50 emails/week from Sales Navigator exports**

## List Sourcing: Sales Navigator → Lix → CSV

### Process
1. **Build saved searches in LinkedIn Sales Navigator** for your 4 segments:
   - Segment A: People you've worked with (filter by company history)
   - Segment B: Ex-colleagues (mutual connections + company history)
   - Segment C: Target role/industry (filter: UK + US, advertising agencies, recruitment, fractional operators)
   - Segment D: Broader target list (general geography + industry)

2. **Export using Lix** (Chrome extension integrates with Sales Nav):
   - Export entire list as CSV or Excel
   - Includes: Name, email, company, title, location, LinkedIn URL

3. **Import into Pulse.** (your own tool):
   - Use CSV import functionality
   - Tag each batch with segment (A/B/C/D) for tracking
   - Or use separate campaigns in Pulse. for each segment

### Why This Approach
- ✅ **No paid list required** (Sales Nav is your main cost)
- ✅ **Warm-by-default** (you've hand-curated the list, not random purchase)
- ✅ **Full control** (you decide who to reach out to)
- ✅ **Traceable** (you know exactly why you picked each person)

## Channel Coordination: Email + LinkedIn in Parallel

### Timeline for Same Person (if in your Sales Nav list)

```
Day 0 ──→ You add them in Sales Navigator
         Trigger: Email #1 sent (wait 1-2 days first, let them see your profile)

Day 1-2 ──→ Email #1 lands in their inbox
           Opening depends on segment (A, B, C, or D)

Day 4 ──→ Dripify sends LinkedIn DM #1 (same warm, question-based angle as LinkedIn channel)
         They're now getting both email + DM in parallel

Day 5 ──→ Email #2 lands (follow-up: "Following up on my last email. Curious if it resonated?")

Day 8-9 ──→ Dripify sends LinkedIn DM #2 (soft reminder, references email)

Day 10 ──→ Email #3 lands (final touch: shorter version of Email #1)

Day 13-14 ──→ Dripify sends LinkedIn DM #3 (final, understanding touch)

Anytime they respond (email OR DM) ──→ YOU take over manually
             (pause automation, personal conversation begins)
```

### Key Insight
By Day 4, they've seen your email AND received your DM. This creates **multi-channel presence** without being spammy. You're just hitting them through different channels at different times.

## List Sourcing Details

### Sales Navigator Setup

1. **Create 4 saved searches:**

   **Search A (Personal contacts):**
   - Keywords: Company names you've worked at
   - Filter by connection (1st degree connections only)
   - Sort: Recently updated

   **Search B (Ex-colleagues):**
   - Keywords: Your past companies
   - Filter: 2nd degree connections + mutual connections
   - Role: Any

   **Search C (Target audience – LinkedIn-connected):**
   - Location: UK + US
   - Industry: Advertising, Recruiting, Staffing, Business Services
   - Title: "New Business," "BD," "Growth," "Sales Director," "Owner," "Founder," "Consultant"
   - Company size: 1–1000 employees
   - Exclude people you already follow

   **Search D (Broad cold list):**
   - Location: UK + US
   - Industry: Same as Search C
   - Title: Any of above (less restrictive)
   - Company size: Any

2. **Export using Lix:**
   - Install Lix Chrome extension
   - Click "Export" on your saved search results
   - Choose: CSV or Excel
   - Fields exported: Name, Email, Company, Title, Location, LinkedIn URL

3. **Import into Pulse.:**
   - Go to Campaigns → Import CSV
   - Paste the Lix export
   - Map columns: Name, Email, Company, Title
   - Tag segment: A / B / C / D
   - Create separate campaign OR add to master campaign with segment column

## Decision Rules & Optimization

### If Email Open Rate <15%

**Diagnosis:** Subject line or sender reputation issue (or emails going to spam).

**Action:**
1. Check spam folder (are emails landing there?)
2. Test new email opening (try more specific, less generic)
3. If still low, try 1-day wait instead of 1-2 days (send sooner)
4. Consider warming up email domain (use Lemwarm or similar)

### If Click Rate <2%

**Diagnosis:** CTA or link placement isn't clear.

**Action:**
1. Move link higher in email (to first paragraph)
2. Try: "Free beta: [link]" instead of just "[link]"
3. Or test: "Check it out: [link]"
4. Add CTA copy: "Test it free" instead of bare link

### If Reply Rate <3%

**Diagnosis:** Opening copy isn't hitting the pain point.

**Action:**
1. Test new Segment D opening: Lead with bigger pain point
2. Or test: "Most people doing your role struggle with X. I built Pulse. to fix that."
3. Try adding specificity: "Most BD directors I talk to say follow-ups are their biggest blocker."
4. Measure by segment: If C (LinkedIn-connected) replies more than D, maybe D copy is off

### If Segment A/B (Warm) Converts Much Better Than C/D (Cold)

**Diagnosis:** Relationship temperature matters.

**Action:**
1. Increase volume in A/B (they're warm, go harder)
2. Pause or reduce Segment D (cold isn't working)
3. Focus on: Building warm list from Sales Nav (your network)
4. Use cold email as secondary, not primary

### If Zero Signups After 2 Weeks

**Diagnosis:** Something structural is broken (wrong audience, bad CTA, or email deliverability).

**Action:**
1. Pull 3–5 recent replies (if any)
2. Do 1:1 call with someone who clicked but didn't sign up (diagnose friction)
3. Check: Are emails landing in inbox or spam?
4. Test: Different opening, different CTA, different subject line
5. Consider: Wrong audience, or product isn't resonating?

### If Segment Performs >5% Reply Rate

**Diagnosis:** This segment is working! Compound it.

**Action:**
1. Increase volume: 10 emails/week → 20 emails/week from this segment
2. Don't change the opening (it's working)
3. Keep the 3-email sequence intact
4. Scale to fill your 50/week budget with what's working

## Notes & Future Iterations

- **Segment Temperature:** A (personal) will always convert better than D (cold). Don't be surprised. Use this insight to prioritize your time.
- **List Freshness:** Re-export from Sales Navigator every 1–2 weeks to add new people. Don't send to the same person twice.
- **Channel Overlap:** By running LinkedIn + email in parallel, you're maximizing touchpoints. Some people will respond to email; some to DM. Both are wins.
- **Scaling:** Once you have a winning segment (e.g., Segment C converts at 5%+ reply rate), you can scale it aggressively.
- **Personalization Note:** You chose "zero personalization" on email, but segment-specific openings ARE personalization (by relationship temperature). This is the right balance—personal enough to feel warm, generic enough to scale.
